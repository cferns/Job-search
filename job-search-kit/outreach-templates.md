## H1B / sponsorship framing — say it the RIGHT way

**Where:** with the recruiter, once there's mutual interest. **Never** in a cover letter or
first cold line to a hiring manager.

**Lead with "authorized + continuing," not "I need a visa":**

> "I'm currently authorized to work in the US and looking for an employer to continue
> sponsorship. Is this role able to support that?"

**If asked the standard form question** "Will you now or in the future require sponsorship?"
answer honestly (**Yes**) — your profile.yaml is set to do this automatically. Don't
over-explain on a form.

**If a recruiter says they can't sponsor:** thank them, ask if other teams can, and move on
fast — don't spend tailoring effort there. (Verify sponsors on h1bgrader.com first; see
`agent/h1b-sponsorship.md`.)
