## STAR behavioral stories

### 1. Drive results at scale — MLOps acceleration
*(Tests: ownership, execution, impact)*
- **S:** Target's ML teams took 6+ months to get a model into production; the platform and
  process were the bottleneck across dozens of teams.
- **T:** As Lead TPM for the enterprise MLOps program, I owned cutting time-to-production
  and scaling adoption org-wide.
- **A:** Defined the program strategy and MLOps maturity model; standardized onboarding
  (docs, templates, enablement hubs); ran steering/director governance to unblock
  dependencies; built MLOps working groups so teams could ship, monitor, and iterate
  independently.
- **R:** Cut time-to-production from **6+ months to under 1 month** and onboarded **40+
  product teams** — turning a bottleneck into a self-service capability.

### 2. Influence without authority — aligning 400+ stakeholders
*(Tests: leadership, communication, cross-functional)*
- **S:** The MLOps program touched Product, Platform, Data Science, and Leadership — 400+
  people with competing priorities and no single owner.
- **T:** Get them aligned on priorities, milestones, and success metrics without direct
  authority over any of them.
- **A:** Stood up a two-tier governance rhythm (executive steering + working-level syncs);
  translated technical roadmaps into business outcomes for leaders and concrete asks for
  ICs; made tradeoffs visible so decisions happened in the room.
- **R:** Kept a 40+ team program on schedule with **400+ stakeholders** rowing in the same
  direction — measured by on-time milestones and adoption.

### 3. Ambiguity / 0→1 — the intake-to-delivery playbook
*(Tests: structured thinking, operating-model design)*
- **S:** Data/analytics requests hit Product, Platform, and Users with no shared process —
  "cross-functional chaos," unpredictable delivery.
- **T:** Create order from scratch so use cases shipped predictably.
- **A:** Designed and operationalized an intake-to-delivery playbook end to end — intake,
  prioritization, hand-offs, and delivery — and drove adoption across teams.
- **R:** Replaced ad-hoc chaos with **predictable, accelerated delivery** of data/analytics
  use cases across the org.

### 4. High-risk execution — decommissioning legacy at enterprise scale
*(Tests: risk management, change management, stakeholder care)*
- **S:** Target was running on 4+ legacy tools (2 reporting, 1 storage, 1 automation) that
  had to be retired while 615 directors depended on them daily.
- **T:** Migrate everyone to modern data management + consumption/visualization platforms
  without breaking the business.
- **A:** Sequenced the decommissioning; partnered with platform/governance on standards;
  built data-literacy onboarding so users landed softly; managed risk and comms for a
  population including 40+ Data Science directors.
- **R:** Migrating **615 directors** off legacy tooling onto modern platforms — a
  high-stakes change executed without disrupting enterprise reporting.

### 5. Product sense + business impact — ML recommendation engine
*(Tests: product judgment, prioritization, customer/revenue focus)*
- **S:** Target wanted more value from its ML-powered recommendations.
- **T:** As PM, own the roadmap and strategy for the recommendation engine.
- **A:** Partnered with 8+ scientists and engineers to improve ranking models; ran backlog
  prioritization and cross-functional validation cycles to ship changes that mattered.
- **R:** Drove **$25M+ in attributable demand** through ranking improvements.

### 6. Speed under pressure — GenAI MVPs for Marketing
*(Tests: bias to action, GenAI fluency)*
- **S:** Marketing needed AI-generated text, image, and metadata fast.
- **T:** Define and launch MVPs and align Creative, Product, and Data Science.
- **A:** Scoped the MVPs, drove structured stakeholder alignment, and shipped in weeks.
- **R:** **$1M+ projected impact** from AI workflow optimization.

### 7. Disagreement / tradeoff *(prepare in your own words)*
Pick a real moment — e.g. pushing back on a low-value request during prioritization, or a
build-vs-buy call (your Alteryx/Dataiku/Knime competitive analysis is a great example of
recommending the *right* tool over the loud one). Frame: the disagreement, how you used
data to align, the outcome, what you learned.

## Question bank to drill

**Behavioral (have a STAR ready for each):** biggest impact · influenced without authority ·
handled ambiguity · a project that failed / what you learned · managed a difficult
stakeholder · made a tough prioritization call · drove a cross-functional initiative.

**Execution / program management:** How do you run a program with 40+ teams? · How do you
track and report status to executives? · How do you manage cross-team dependencies and risk?
· How do you handle a slipping milestone? · How do you define success metrics for a platform?

**Product sense:** How would you measure success of an internal ML platform? · How do you
prioritize a platform roadmap when every team wants something different? · Build vs. buy for
a data tool — walk me through it (use Alteryx/Dataiku/Knime). · How do you drive adoption of
an internal tool?

**Metrics / analytics:** What's your North Star for an MLOps platform? (e.g. time-to-prod,
# models in prod, adoption, reliability) · How do you tie platform work to business value? ·
What would you instrument first?

**Data/ML platform "system design" (lightweight):** Walk through the lifecycle from data →
model → production → monitoring. Where are the failure points? How would you design intake,
governance, and self-service? Lean on your real MLOps maturity model.
