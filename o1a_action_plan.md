## Why O-1A is the anchor

1. **Best "operate-now" route for a solo founder** — no prevailing-wage requirement,
   no cap/lottery, you run your own company (your record, not your org chart, is judged).
2. **Evidence ports to EB-1A** — and EB-1A is where your **Jan 2024 priority date**
   plugs in. So O-1A evidence literally becomes your green card.
3. **Nothing is wasted** — every validation, launch, press hit, and talk doubles as
   O-1A evidence. You're testing the app regardless → you're building the case regardless.

## The bar

**O-1A = "extraordinary ability": sustained national/international acclaim, among the
small percentage at the very top of the field.** You must satisfy **≥3 of 8 criteria**
(target **5–6** for safety) plus a convincing overall narrative ("final merits").

Petitioner can be a **U.S. employer or agent** — your own company can petition
(post-2024 USCIS guidance is founder-friendly). Requires a **peer/expert advisory
opinion** (or justified waiver). Validity: **up to 3 years, renewable in 1-year
increments** (no 6-year cap like H-1B).

## Start NOW (free/cheap, no status change needed)

1. **Judging** — apply to judge 2–3 hackathons/pitch comps this quarter (Devpost,
   university accelerators, local startup events). Cheapest criterion; do it first.
2. **Authorship** — publish ~1 article/month on the problem your app solves; pitch
   meetup/conference talks.
3. **Provisional patent** — if the app has anything novel, file a provisional (cheap)
   → feeds "original contributions."
4. **Press list** — build a target list of journalists/podcasts now, ready to pitch at
   beta/launch.
5. **Brag file** — log every metric, mention, signup, and milestone from day one.

## The two pillars people underestimate

- **Recommendation letters (5–8)** from recognized experts — *independent* writers
  (who don't know you personally) carry the most weight. Start warming up these
  relationships **now**; they take months.
- **Advisory opinion / peer consultation** — required for O-1A (or a justified waiver).
  Your attorney handles it, but plan for it.

## Compliance guardrails (don't jeopardize your status / I-140)

- On your **current corporate H-1B** you may **own** the company and do **market
  research** (interviews, waitlist, pre-orders, demo feedback) — but you **cannot
  actively operate/run the product** (e.g., a 90+ day live beta) until you have work
  authorization for the startup (**concurrent H-1B**).
- **"Free" does not make operating it legal** — it's your *labor*, not revenue, that
  matters.
- **Don't resign** until your next status is approved (or, for concurrent, confirm with
  Kamil whether **H-1B portability** lets you begin on filing/receipt).
- **Check your employer's contract** (moonlighting + **IP-assignment / PIIA**) before
  filing anything — build strictly on **personal time/equipment** and keep records.
- Your approved, 180-day-seasoned **I-140 is a safety net** (priority date + 3-year
  extensions survive employer withdrawal) — protect it; don't gamble it on gray-zone work.
